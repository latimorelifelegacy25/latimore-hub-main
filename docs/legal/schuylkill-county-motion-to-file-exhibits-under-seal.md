# MEMORANDUM OF LAW IN SUPPORT OF PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO FILE EXHIBITS UNDER SEAL

## I. INTRODUCTION

Plaintiff Jackson M. Latimore, Sr. seeks leave to file two dependency adjudication orders as sealed exhibits in support of his civil complaint against Defendants Assumption BVM School, the Diocese of Allentown, and Principal Carol Boyer. These exhibits are records of juvenile court proceedings subject to the confidentiality protections of Pennsylvania law. Because the public interest in protecting the privacy of minor children in dependency proceedings is well established, and because sealing these exhibits will not prejudice any party, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court grant the motion and permit the exhibits to be filed under seal.

## II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

This civil action arises from Defendants' repeated unauthorized releases of Plaintiff's minor children to a non-custodial parent in alleged violation of a controlling custody order, despite Plaintiff's repeated written directives, Boyer's own written admissions, and the availability of verification resources including a named Children and Youth Services caseworker.

On February 23, 2026, the Schuylkill County Juvenile Court adjudicated Plaintiff's minor children Jaclyn C. Latimore and Jackson M. Latimore, Jr. dependent in the matters of CP-54-DP-0000045-2025 and CP-54-DP-0000046-2025 respectively. Judge James P. Goodman found by clear and convincing evidence that the children were without proper care, control, and safe housing, and vested custody in Father.

These adjudication orders are directly material to this civil action. Plaintiff alleges that Defendants' conduct facilitated the conditions the Juvenile Court later found to be unsafe. The adjudication orders constitute independent judicial corroboration of the substance of the warnings Plaintiff communicated to Defendants throughout the October 2025 through February 2026 period.

## III. LEGAL STANDARD

Pennsylvania law imposes a general prohibition on the public disclosure of juvenile court records. Section 6307 of the Juvenile Act provides:

> "Except as otherwise provided in this chapter, neither the child nor the details of the hearing before the court . . . shall be disclosed to the public."

42 Pa.C.S. § 6307. The statute reflects a longstanding legislative judgment that the privacy of minor children involved in dependency proceedings warrants protection from public exposure.

Pa.R.J.C.P. 160 similarly restricts access to juvenile court records and requires that any disclosure in a non-juvenile proceeding be authorized by the court. The rule reflects the principle that juvenile records should not migrate into public civil court files without judicial oversight.

Courts addressing the use of juvenile records in civil proceedings have recognized that the confidentiality interest of the minor children must be balanced against the legitimate evidentiary needs of the civil litigant. Where, as here, the juvenile records are directly material to the civil claims and sealing adequately protects the children's privacy interests, courts have consistently permitted use of such records under seal. See generally *In re J.D.*, 798 A.2d 210 (Pa. Super. 2002) (recognizing the primacy of the child's privacy interest in juvenile proceedings).

## IV. ARGUMENT

### A. The Dependency Adjudication Orders Are Directly Material to Plaintiff's Civil Claims.

Plaintiff's civil complaint alleges that Defendants were on repeated written notice of child welfare concerns yet continued releasing the minor children to a non-custodial parent in violation of a court order. The dependency adjudication orders entered on February 23, 2026 constitute judicial findings by clear and convincing evidence that the conditions Plaintiff warned Defendants about were real, foreseeable, and harmful.

Without these exhibits, Plaintiff's allegations regarding the consequences of Defendants' conduct rest on his own assertions. With them, those allegations are corroborated by an independent judicial determination. The exhibits are not merely cumulative — they are the single most probative items in the record regarding the foreseeability and actual occurrence of harm.

### B. Sealing the Exhibits Adequately Protects the Children's Privacy Interests.

The confidentiality provisions of § 6307 and Pa.R.J.C.P. 160 exist to protect minor children from the public stigma and reputational harm that can result from exposure of dependency proceedings. These interests are fully served by sealing the exhibits in this civil matter. Sealing restricts public access to the docket entry while permitting the Court and the parties to use the records in the litigation.

The children's identities are already referenced in the public complaint by initials and birth years consistent with Pennsylvania civil court practice regarding minor children. Sealing Exhibits P-15 and P-16 ensures that the full adjudication orders, which contain more detailed identifying information, are not accessible to the general public through the civil docket.

### C. No Less Restrictive Alternative Adequately Serves Both Interests.

Plaintiff has considered whether redaction alone would be sufficient. However, because the adjudication orders identify the children by full name and date of birth and reference specific docket numbers in the juvenile system, redaction would undermine the foundational authenticity of the exhibits without meaningfully reducing the privacy concern addressed by sealing. Sealing is the appropriate and well-established mechanism under Pennsylvania law for this situation.

### D. No Party Will Be Prejudiced by Sealing.

Defendants will receive full copies of Exhibits P-15 and P-16 as part of service of the Complaint. Sealing restricts public access, not party access. Defendants will have full opportunity to review, challenge, and respond to these exhibits in the course of litigation. No legitimate interest of any party is impaired by the sealing order Plaintiff requests.

## V. CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Honorable Court enter an Order permitting Exhibits P-15 and P-16 to be filed under seal, restricting public access to those exhibits on the civil docket, and permitting the parties and the Court to access the exhibits in connection with this litigation.
